When a creator launches their token on Matiz, they don't need to round up
investors to "provide liquidity" or rely on an exchange. Liquidity is already
guaranteed by the math. But how?

## The social-token problem

Earlier categories of social tokens died for one simple reason: **nobody could
sell**. If you buy a creator's token and want to exit tomorrow, you need someone
else to buy it from you. Without a buyer, your token is practically worth zero —
no matter how popular the creator still is.

## The bonding curve as a market maker

A bonding curve is an automated market maker: the token price is defined by a
function of the circulating supply. At Matiz we use:

> **P = S^0.5**

where `P` is the price in USDC and `S` is the supply. Every purchase pushes the
price up; every sale pushes it down. There's no order book and no counterparty:
the curve **always** buys back from you.

## The reserve always covers it

The elegant part is that the reserve —the deposited USDC— is exactly the area
under the curve:

> **R = (2/3) · S^1.5**

This guarantees, by mathematical construction, that if every holder sold at the
same time, the reserve covers it exactly. It's not a promise: it's a property.
